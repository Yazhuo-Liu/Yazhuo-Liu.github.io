Decoupe the entropic energy and enthalpic Energy

Assuming that the deformation of the hyperelastic solid can be divided into two steps: The first step is purely entropic, which means only the orientation of the carbon-carbon bond is changed and the length of each atomic bond is fixed so that the volume is conserved. And the second step is purely enthalpic, which means the polymer configuration (relative directions of all atoms) is unchanged and only the bond length is stretched. It is reasonable to assume the second step satisfies the linear elasticity and and only it contributes to material fracture.

Since the change of bond length is only reflected in the enthalpic step, we can set the fracture criterion as the material fracture after the enthalpy strain energy reaches a certain threshold. We then implemented the above modeling with the Phase-field method and calculated the results.
